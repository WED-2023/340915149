Answers to frequently asked questions about my struggles with identity, purpose, and web development:


Q: List three main differences between web1, web2 and web3.

A:

Ownership and Control:

  • Web1: Static, read-only websites with centralized hosting.
  • Web2: Interactive, user-generated content with centralized control by platform providers.
  • Web3: Decentralized using blockchain, with data ownership distributed among users.

Technology and Interaction:

  • Web1: Basic HTML, minimal interaction.
  • Web2: Advanced technologies like AJAX and JavaScript enable interactive experiences.
  • Web3: Utilizes blockchain and decentralized apps for peer-to-peer interactions.

Economic Model:

  • Web1: Monetization through advertising and subscriptions.
  • Web2: Targeted advertising and data-driven monetization strategies.
  • Web3: Token-based economies where users can earn and have financial stakes.

Q: Explain the differences stated in question 1 by an example of a use case

A: For the query "how to make soap":
  • Web1: A static webpage detailing a basic soap-making process.
  • Web2: Interactive tutorials on platforms like YouTube, with comments and user-generated tips.
  • Web3: A decentralized platform where users share soap-making techniques, validated by the community, with contributors earning tokens for quality contributions.
